### 1. LongStraw: Long-Context RL Beyond 2M Tokens under a Fixed GPU Budget
**arXiv:2607.14952** | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14952) | [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.14952)

**Authors:** Changhai Zhou, Kieran Liu, Yuhua Zhou, Qian Qiao, Jun Gao, Harry Zhang, Irvine Lu, Nolan Ho, Lucian Li, Andrew Lei, Cleon Cheng, Steven Chiang, Yihang Zeng, Di Zhang, Rio Yang, Kaijie Chen, Andrew Chen, Pony Ma, Weizhong Zhang, Cheng Jin

**Abstract:** A growing gap separates inference context lengths from RL post-training: inference systems are approaching million-token contexts, while post-training workloads often remain at 256K tokens or below and rely on length generalization at deployment. The gap is especially important for AI agents, whose observations, tool outputs, documents, and prior decisions accumulate over long trajectories. LongStraw bridges this gap by enabling long-context RL under a fixed GPU budget, proposing techniques for memory-efficient training that supports beyond 2M token contexts.

> **中文摘要：** 推理系统的上下文长度已接近百万 token，而 RL 后训练通常仍限于 256K token 且依赖长度泛化。LongStraw 在固定 GPU 预算下实现长上下文 RL 训练，支持超过 200 万 token 的上下文长度，对 AI Agent 长轨迹训练尤为关键。

### 2. HeteroMosaic: Exposing and Exploiting Heterogeneous Execution Opportunities for Energy-Efficient Edge LLM Inference
**arXiv:2607.12839** | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12839) | [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.12839)

**Authors:** (Not available in parsed data)

**Abstract:** Modern edge system-on-chips (SoCs) combine CPUs, integrated GPUs (iGPUs), and neural processing units (NPUs), yet existing LLM runtimes typically make coarse device-level decisions or optimize operators in isolation. ### 9. DRIFT: Direct Reduced Fourier Transforms for Distributed Spectral Neural Operators
**arXiv:2607.14394** | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14394) | [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.14394)

**Authors:** Sana Taghipour Anvari, David Kaeli

**Abstract:** Fourier Neural Operators (FNOs) scaling to high-resolution grids requires distributing data across GPUs, but the distributed FFT at the core of each spectral layer requires multiple dense all-to-all collectives. DRIFT introduces the Distributed Truncated Spectral Transform (DTST), where each GPU computes only a small subset of frequency modes locally, reducing communication from 97% to under 6% of forward-pass time with 38-64x forward-pass speedup on 4-32 GPUs.

> **中文摘要：** 分布式 FNO 训练中全对全通信是主要瓶颈。DRIFT 的分布式截断谱变换仅需在局部计算少量频率模式，将通信占比从 97% 降至 6% 以下，4-32 GPU 上前向传播加速 38-64 倍。

**arXiv:2607.14618** | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14618) | [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.14618)

**Abstract:** CPUs are the most universal target for on-device LLM inference, but existing low-bit quantization methods offer either coarse operating points or fine-grained mixed precision that is difficult to execute efficiently on CPUs. ### 19. Don't Predict, Prioritize: Rethinking GPU Reliability Assessment
**arXiv:2607.15115** | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15115) | [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.15115)

**Authors:** Difeng Ma, Changhua Pei, Yuanwei Lu, Quan Zhou, Zexin Wang, Yibo Zhu, Daxin Jiang, Dan Pei, Jingjing Li, Gaogang Xie

**Abstract:** The reliability of GPUs is a critical bottleneck for modern large-scale AI infrastructure, where a single node failure can disrupt synchronous training jobs and cause significant financial losses. Through analysis of telemetry data from a production cluster, the authors find that major GPU failures exhibit strong stochasticity, making time-based prediction ineffective. They propose HeaRank (Health Rank), a Learning-to-Rank framework that computes a global risk ranking of GPU nodes. Deployed on a production cluster with thousands of GPUs, it achieves AUC of 0.83 and captures 64% of future failures within the top 5% of ranked nodes.

> **中文摘要：** GPU 可靠性是 AI 基础设施的关键瓶颈。HeaRank 采用 Learning-to-Rank 方法对 GPU 节点进行故障风险排序，在生产级数千 GPU 集群上 AUC 达 0.83，前 5% 高风险节点捕捉到 64% 的未来故障。被 KDD 2026 接收。

### 20. The Cost and Network Limits of Space-Based AI Compute
**arXiv:2607.14172** | [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14172) | [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.14172)

**Authors:** Kees van Berkel

**Abstract:** This paper evaluates whether large-scale AI data centers deployed in low-Earth orbit (LEO) could become a cost-effective alternative to terrestrial facilities. The analysis compares orbital and ground-based systems across launch cost, power generation, cooling, radiation exposure, and compute-network performance. Using bisection bandwidth, bisection intensity, and roofline-style models, the paper shows that while LEO-based inference may be feasible, training frontier-scale LLMs in orbit is unlikely to be competitive.

> **中文摘要：** 评估近地轨道部署大规模 AI 数据中心的可行性。对比发射成本、发电、散热和网络性能后得出结论：轨道推理可能可行，但训练前沿大模型不太可能超过地面数据中心。
