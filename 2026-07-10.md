### A Practical Investigation of Training-free Relaxed Speculative Decoding

- **ArXiv:** [`2607.08690v1`](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08690v1) | **PDF:** [📄](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08690v1)
- **作者:** Yuntong Hu, Shaojie Jiang, Shuangrui Ding, Yuchen Hu, Siqi Ma, Ruiyang Qin, Michael Zhang, Ling Liu, Weitao Xu
- **标签:** `cs.LG, cs.AI`

**中文摘要：**

本文系统性地研究了免训练宽松投机解码（Relaxed Speculative Decoding, RSD）方法的实际效果。RSD通过放宽验证标准来接受更多草稿令牌，从而获得2-3倍加速，但这种加速以引入采样偏差为代价。实验发现：RSD在代码生成和摘要等结构化任务上加速效果最显著，在知识密集型任务上增益有限，在创意写作上几乎无提升。研究表明RSD的实际收益高度任务依赖，部署时需根据具体场景校准验证标准。

**English Abstract:**

Speculative decoding accelerates sampling from an autoregressive LLM by using a faster auxiliary model to draft tokens which are then verified in parallel by the LLM. Standard speculative decoding is lossless: its rejection and resampling steps exactly preserve the LLM's sampling distribution. Recent work has proposed relaxed verification criteria that accept more draft tokens at the cost of introducing sampling bias, often reporting 2-3x speedups over standard speculative decoding on summarization and code generation tasks. However, it remains unclear whether these speedups generalize to open-ended tasks and how they trade off against distributional fidelity. In this paper, we systematically evaluate Relaxed Speculative Decoding (RSD) under six verification criteria across diverse tasks. We find that while RSD consistently improves token acceptance and wall-clock speed, its effectiveness varies substantially by task: the largest speedups occur on structured tasks (code generation, summarization), while gains are marginal on knowledge-intensive tasks and negligible on creative writing. We further show that relaxing the verification criterion does not proportionally prune low-quality tokens and that RSD requires considerably more decoding rounds per token to approach the target distribution. These results suggest that RSD's practical benefits are task-dependent and practitioners should calibrate verification criteria based on the deployment scenario.

### DominoTree: Conditional Tree-Structured Drafting with Domino for Speculative Decoding

- **ArXiv:** [`2607.08642v1`](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08642v1) | **PDF:** [📄](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08642v1)
- **作者:** Ziyi Yang, Tianyu Pang, Dongchen Han, Chao Du, Hanxiao Zhang, Qiang Su, Huazhu Fu, Chongxuan Li, Yuan Yao, Xianyuan Zhan
- **标签:** `cs.CL`

**中文摘要：**

本文提出DominoTree，一种新型投机解码起草器。现有方法（DFlash的块扩散草稿器、DDTree的最佳优先树方法）忽略了草稿令牌之间的条件依赖关系。DominoTree通过分治策略结合概率前缀树（probabilistic trie）对草稿令牌间的条件联合分布进行建模，有效捕捉了所有可能的令牌序列。在多个基准上，以7B规模目标模型取得了高达2.14倍加速，超过DFlash和DDTree分别达1.77倍和1.28倍。在更大模型上，Llama-3.1-8B达到3.46倍加速。

**English Abstract:**

Speculative decoding accelerates LLM inference by drafting several tokens and verifying them in parallel. Block-diffusion drafters such as DFlash produce a draft block in one pass but model only per-position marginals; best-first tree methods such as DDTree expand candidate trees from those marginals with heuristic schedules that ignore conditional dependencies among positions. We observe that when drafting multiple tokens, the marginal probability at earlier positions is influenced by the probability assignments at later positions, and vice versa -- a phenomenon we call the interdependence among speculative tokens. This motivates DominoTree, a novel drafter that models conditional joint distributions among speculative tokens using a divide-and-conquer strategy combined with a probabilistic trie. The probabilistic trie compactly captures all possible token sequences, and the divide-and-conquer strategy recursively estimates joint probabilities over subtrees. On multiple benchmarks, DominoTree with a 7B-sized target model achieves up to 2.14x speedup, outperforming DFlash and DDTree by up to 1.77x and 1.28x, respectively. Additionally, when scaling to larger models, DominoTree achieves up to 2.50x speedup on GPT-2-1.5B and up to 3.46x speedup on Llama-3.1-8B. Code is available at https://github.com/tmlr-group/DominoTree.

### The Illusion of Equivalency: Statistical Characterization of Quantization Effects in LLMs

- **ArXiv:** [`2607.08734v1`](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08734v1) | **PDF:** [📄](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08734v1)
- **作者:** Rui Li, Yuren Mao, Chunjing Xiao, Xiaojun Ye, Wenjia Lei, Wei Wang, Shi Feng
- **标签:** `cs.AI`

**中文摘要：**

本文揭示了现有LLM量化评估方法的重大缺陷。虽然量化后的模型在准确率和困惑度（perplexity）指标上表现良好，但这些指标无法捕捉量化导致的模型行为变化。研究46个量化LLM后发现：(1) 量化模型虽保持>99%的正确率一致性，但输出内容差异可达29%；(2) 提示策略会放大量化引起的不稳定性；(3) 现有校准方法只能缓解而非消除这些影响。该工作强调了在部署流程中需要更全面的量化模型行为评估。

**English Abstract:**

Post-training quantization is widely used to deploy large language models in resource-constrained settings, yet its evaluation relies almost exclusively on accuracy and perplexity. We show that these metrics fail to capture behavioral changes induced by quantization. We introduce correctness agreement rate, perplexity degradation, and utterance consistency as complementary metrics, and study 46 quantized LLMs across 4 model families, 5 quantization methods, and 3 bit-widths on 8 text-generation tasks. We find that (1) quantized models often achieve near-perfect correctness agreement (>99%) while exhibiting substantial generational diversity (up to 29% difference) in their outputs; (2) prompting strategies amplify quantization-induced instability; (3) existing calibration methods mitigate but do not eliminate these effects. These findings reveal a critical gap in current evaluation practices and underscore the need for richer characterization of quantized model behavior in deployment pipelines.

### BiSCo-LLM: Lookup-Free Binary Spherical Coding for Extreme Low-Bit Large Language Model Compression

- **ArXiv:** [`2607.08643v1`](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08643v1) | **PDF:** [📄](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08643v1)
- **作者:** Peijie Dong, Lujun Li, Zhenheng Tang, Xiang Fei, Xiaowen Chu, Yike Guo
- **标签:** `cs.LG`

**中文摘要：**

本文提出BiSCo-LLM，一种免查表的二值球面编码框架，用于极端低位LLM压缩。现有的二值编码方法虽能保持表示能力，但推理时需要昂贵的码本查找（codebook lookup），引入额外开销。BiSCo-LLM通过联合优化编码过程与球面码本的闭式投影，消除了在线码本搜索，将推理重构为标准矩阵乘法，兼容现有线性代数原语。在2-bit和1.5-bit量化水平下，LLaMA-2/3、Qwen2.5和DeepSeek2模型均能保持极小精度损失，显著优于QuaRot等最先进量化方法。在2-bit下，LLaMA-3-8B的WikiText-2困惑度提升76.49。

**English Abstract:**

Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly constrained by memory capacity, weight bandwidth, and checkpoint storage during deployment. Existing low-bit compression methods mainly follow two directions. Scalar or group-wise quantization is simple and compatible with efficient low-precision kernels on GPUs, but packing weights into very low bits (e.g., 2 bits) causes severe accuracy degradation. Alternatively, binary coding decomposes weights into linear combinations of binary vectors to preserve representational capacity, yet requires an expensive codebook lookup during inference that introduces non-trivial overhead. We present BiSCo-LLM, a lookup-free binary spherical coding framework that achieves extreme LLM compression while maintaining deployment efficiency. BiSCo-LLM eliminates the online codebook search by jointly optimizing the encoding process with a closed-form projection onto a spherical codebook, and reformulates inference as standard matrix multiplications compatible with existing linear algebra primitives. Extensive experiments show that BiSCo-LLM compresses LLaMA-2/3, Owen2.5, and DeepSeek2 to 2-bit and 1.5-bit levels with negligible accuracy loss, outperforming state-of-the-art quantization and binary coding methods. Specifically, at the 2-bit level, BiSCo-LLM outperforms the leading quantization method QuaRot by an average WikiText-2 perplexity improvement of 76.49 and 41.21 for LLaMA-3-8B and LLaMA-3-70B respectively. Code is available at https://github.com/DD-DuDa/BiSCo-LLM.

### It Takes a MAESTRO To Prune Bad Experts

- **ArXiv:** [`2607.08601v1`](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08601v1) | **PDF:** [📄](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08601v1)
- **作者:** Mohammed Nowaz Rabbani Sowrote, Marco Federici, Mahdi Beitollahi, Dongfang Liu, Muhammad Jehangir, Sayantan Datta, Rui Li
- **标签:** `cs.CL`

**中文摘要：**

本文提出MAESTRO，一种面向MoE（混合专家）语言模型的训练感知结构化剪枝框架。MoE模型虽激活参数少，但所有专家常驻内存形成部署瓶颈。现有方法要么忽略令牌级路由统计直接剪枝，要么忽视预训练期间的专家重要性。MAESTRO通过滑动窗口门控分析追踪预训练期间的专家使用模式，将专家剪枝建模为带内存预算的约束优化问题。实验表明，在MMLU、ARC等基准上，MAESTRO可剪枝高达40%的专家，同时保留98%以上的原始性能，显著超越现有剪枝方法。该方法架构无关，适用于任何MoE架构。

**English Abstract:**

Sparsely-activated Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) language models achieve remarkable inference efficiency by activating only a small fraction of parameters per token, yet their full expert banks reside in memory at all times, creating a prohibitive deployment bottleneck. Existing structured pruning methods either prune entire experts from the expert bank regardless of token-level routing statistics, or rely on post-training routing behavior that neglects expert importance during pre-training. We introduce MAESTRO (Memory-Aware Expert Selection through Training-aware Retention and Operation), a training-aware structured pruning framework that identifies and prunes redundant experts by leveraging dynamic expert importance scores computed during pre-training, while incorporating a budgeted memory constraint. During pre-training, we track expert utilization patterns through a sliding-window gating analysis. After pre-training, we formulate expert pruning as a constrained optimization: given a memory budget, select experts to retain that best preserve model capability. Extensive experiments on datasets including MMLU, ARC, HellaSwag, and GSM8K demonstrate that MAESTRO can prune up to 40% of experts while retaining more than 98% of the original performance, and surpasses existing pruning methods by significant margins. Our approach is architecture-agnostic and applicable to any MoE architecture, making it a versatile tool for efficient MoE deployment.

### On the Limitations of Non-GPU AI Accelerators for Large-Model Inference: A Field Study of MoE and Multimodal Workloads

- **ArXiv:** [`2607.08215v1`](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08215v1) | **PDF:** [📄](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08215v1)
- **作者:** Zhongyin Deng, Yiyang Chen, Ziyue Yang, Dongsheng Li, Mingxing Hu, Jinkui Hu, Xiyang Wang, Xiaobo Zhou
- **标签:** `cs.DC`

**中文摘要：**

本文首次公开报告了在商用RISC-V NPU（神经网络处理器）上部署大规模MoE和多模态推理工作负载的工程实践。在16设备集群上，研究发现NPU的架构限制（有限的片上内存、低内存带宽、小的TLB覆盖范围和浅层片上互连）导致三个关键系统级瓶颈：内存受限的内核执行、MoE路由器的非规则内存访问模式以及跨NPU通信开销。实验表明，MoE推理中的all-to-all通信在4-NPU配置下占端到端延迟的40%，内核启动开销占11-30%。该研究为新兴的非GPU推理生态系统中的软硬件协同设计提供了实用指南。

**English Abstract:**

Non-GPU AI accelerators are increasingly adopted as alternatives to general-purpose GPUs for large-model inference, but the real engineering cost of migrating demanding workloads beyond CUDA remains poorly documented. We present a field study of deploying two large inference workloads on a 16-device cluster of a commercial RISC-V NPU: a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) language model with up to 47B activated parameters and a multimodal generative model with 8B image encoder and 8B language decoder. We document concrete engineering challenges introduced by the NPU's architectural constraints, including its limited on-chip memory, low memory bandwidth, small TLB reach, and shallow on-chip interconnect. We identify three key system-level bottlenecks -- memory-bound kernel execution, irregular memory access patterns introduced by the MoE router, and cross-NPU communication overhead -- and characterize their impact on end-to-end inference throughput across model scales and batch sizes. Our measurements reveal that the all-to-all communication in MoE inference on this NPU takes up to 40% of the end-to-end latency in a 4-NPU configuration and kernel launch overhead accounts for another 11-30%. We further investigate workload-specific bottlenecks unique to multimodal generation. This study provides the first public engineering characterization of running large MoE models on a commercial RISC-V AI accelerator, offering practical guidelines for hardware-software co-design in the emerging non-GPU inference ecosystem.
